Culinary Arts & Heritage

From Baltistan to Big Apple Burgers

My cooking journey blends the traditional, soulful flavors of my Balti heritage with the speed and precision of professional European kitchens. Food is my other code.

Traditional Balti Cuisine

My earliest cooking memories are rooted in the traditional mountain cuisine of Baltistan. These dishes are rich, hearty, and meticulously preparedâ€”a perfect reflection of the challenging environment they come from. They represent skill, patience, and community.

Prapu

Cooked barley flour served with a rich sauce made from grinded apricot seeds.

Marzan

A heavy, nutrient-dense meal of cooked barley flour, traditionally served with pure apricot oil or melted butter.

Balay

The traditional winter soup, featuring diverse grains, noodles, and rich animal fats.

Namkeen Chai

The traditional savory pink tea, slowly brewed with milk, cardamom, and a touch of salt.

Mastering the Flavors of Lahore

My time in Lahore, the ultimate food hub, dramatically expanded my palate and skill set. I learned to balance complex spice profiles for a variety of beloved dishes, from robust breakfasts to signature celebratory feasts.

Lahori Chany

The quintessential Lahori breakfast: slow-cooked chickpeas with rich, tangy spices, often served with crispy pooris.

Pulao

A flavorful rice dish where basmati rice is cooked in a rich stock with meat and whole spices, celebrated for its subtle aroma.

Biryani

The festive, complex layered rice dish, featuring marinated meat, vibrant spices, potatoes, and a signature spicy kick.

Karhai

A quick-cooked stir-fry of meat (usually chicken or mutton) prepared in an open wok with fresh ginger, tomatoes, and chilies.

Lahori Takatak

A street food specialty involving minced offal cooked on a large griddle, named after the sound of the cooking spatula.
